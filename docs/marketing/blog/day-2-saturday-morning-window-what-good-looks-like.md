# Day 2, 08:18 CET: What "Doing Well" Looks Like on a Saturday

*Published: Saturday March 28, 2026 | Best platform: Indie Hackers, Substack | Day 2 morning*

---

It's Saturday morning in Stockholm. I launched an AI sketch tool yesterday.

The traffic graphs look like a mountain range with valleys. Product Hunt peaked around noon CET. Hacker News had a wave. Reddit had three. The US afternoon was the second summit. And then at around 23:00 CET — nothing. The internet went to sleep.

This is what Day 2 looks like for a developer tool launched from Europe on a Friday: a quiet Saturday morning, a modest recovery as the US wakes up, and the realization that **launch day is over but the window isn't**.

Here's what I'm looking at right now, and what I think it means.

---

## The Data Points That Matter

I launched to measure three things:
1. **Do AI developers actually want this?** (MCP installs, agent workflow questions)
2. **Can non-developers find value too?** (homeowner, renovation, non-technical signups)
3. **Does the sketch output feel right?** (retention — do people come back after the first sketch?)

Two of those three answered faster than expected. The third is still open.

**Unexpected signal:** About 30% of early signups described themselves as non-developers. They found Skissify via the homeowner renovation angle — describe a room to Claude, get a floor plan sketch, show it to a contractor. That wasn't the pitch. It's now a strategy.

---

## What Day 2 Saturday Means for Product Launches

Friday launches have a hidden advantage: you get two audiences in one day. The EU morning wave and the US afternoon wave are distinct enough to feel like two separate launches. But Saturday has a different texture.

Saturday is quieter. Less competitive. The same MCP developers who were too busy to try your tool on launch day are now in "explore mode" — they have time. Community posts that would have been buried in Friday's noise now breathe.

**The communities that matter most right now:**
- **r/mcp** — 20K highly targeted MCP developers. This is still the top priority.
- **r/LocalLLaMA** — the benchmark data resonates here on weekends
- **r/DnD / r/tabletopgaming** — the dungeon map story travels on Saturday
- **r/HomeImprovement** — homeowners browse on weekends

---

## The Three Things I'd Do Differently

**1. Email capture on Day 0, not Week 2.**
I have no email list. Every visitor who was excited enough to try the editor and leave — gone. Email converts curiosity into something retrievable. I'm fixing this today.

**2. Record the GIF before launch, not after.**
The wobble slider is the most visually compelling thing Skissify does. A 10-second no-narration GIF of the humanness slider going from 0 to 10 would have been shared hundreds of times. I wrote about recording it in 20 cycles of prep notes. I never recorded it. That's a Saturday morning task.

**3. r/mcp on launch day, not Day 2.**
I spent 32 planning cycles producing 55 blog posts and 117 tweets. I forgot to post to the one community that Skissify was literally built for. The r/mcp post goes up today. A day late is still worth it.

---

## The Honest Thing About Launches

You plan for weeks and then the launch takes 24 hours and you're in Day 2 and the intensity is just... gone. The urgency evaporates. The HN thread is stale. Product Hunt is yesterday's list.

What's left is distribution. Not launch distribution — ongoing distribution. The content you write now doesn't have a launch halo, so it has to stand on its own. That's actually a more honest test of whether the product is interesting.

The sketch tool works. The schema is clean. The wobble algorithm produces outputs that make people stop scrolling. Whether it finds its audience at scale depends on what happens in the next 30 days, not the last 24 hours.

Day 2 is less about momentum and more about infrastructure. Build the email list. Post to the right communities. Record the video that was always going to be the best demo.

The launch was yesterday. The product is today.
