---

## What Skissify Is (in case you're new)

Skissify converts JSON into hand-drawn sketches. Floor plans, diagrams, wireframes — anything spatial. AI agents use it via MCP or REST API. Regular humans use it at skissify.com/human — just describe a room in plain text and get a sketch back.

The thing I didn't expect when I launched Friday: homeowners. Not AI developers. Homeowners planning kitchen renovations, using Claude to generate sketches before meeting with contractors. That's the angle I'm leading with on HN.

---

## The Preparation List

### 1. The title (still deciding)

I have five HN title versions written. The one I'm leaning toward:

> **Show HN: Skissify – Describe a room in plain text, get a hand-drawn floor plan sketch**

Why: it leads with the human use case. "AI agents" in the title triggers fatigue. "Describe a room... get a sketch" is concrete, demonstrates itself in the title, and works for both the developer reading it *and* their non-technical partner who they'll show it to.

The runner-up:

> **Show HN: Skissify – JSON or plain text → hand-drawn architectural sketches (MCP + Human Mode)**

This one's more complete but probably too long.

### 2. The first comment

HN convention is to post the maker's comment immediately after submitting. This comment is where you give context the title can't hold. I've drafted mine already. Key elements:
- What problem it solves
- The unexpected audience discovery (homeowners)
- The technical insight (flat JSON → 94% first-try LLM success rate)
- An honest caveat or two
- A concrete question for the community

I'm not going to wing this at 09:05.

### 3. The screenshots

Still need:
- Human Mode: text input → sketch output (30-second flow)
- Blueprint floor plan (best visual first impression)
- Claude Desktop + MCP generating a floor plan

These go in the HN first comment. HN doesn't embed images but you can link to them. Most people check links when they're on the fence.

### 4. The objection responses

Every Show HN gets the same five comments if you don't preempt them:
1. "Why not just use Excalidraw?"
2. "This could be a simple library, why is it a SaaS?"
3. "Isn't this just Mermaid but hand-drawn?"
4. "The pricing seems aggressive for what this is"
5. "Cool demo but what's the actual use case?"

I have answers for all five. Writing them down in advance means I don't spend 15 minutes crafting a careful response while 50 people are reading and forming opinions.

### 5. Newsletter outreach (TODAY)

TLDR AI, The Rundown AI, Ben's Bites — if I contact them before midnight tonight, I *might* make Tuesday's issue. That would mean 500K+ people seeing Skissify the same day HN goes live. The probability is low. The cost is 20 minutes. Worth it.

### 6. Sleep

Possibly the most important item on the list. A founder who's been awake for 22 hours responding to HN comments is a liability. The funny thing about launch day is that the window that matters most — the first 3-4 hours — requires you to be sharp, responsive, and not defensive.

I'm going to sleep before 23:00 CET tonight.

---

## What I'm Not Doing

- **Asking people to upvote**. This violates HN's rules and the spirit of the platform. If the submission is good, it will surface. If it doesn't, that's data.
- **Posting in multiple HN threads simultaneously**. One Show HN is the whole bet.
- **Announcing the HN post before it goes live**. "Going to submit to HN tomorrow, check it out!" generates anti-votes from HN veterans who dislike pre-launch signaling.
- **Over-engineering the copy**. I've written enough versions of the HN title. At some point, picking one and submitting is better than optimizing forever.
