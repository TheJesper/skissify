Launch day is a sprint. Day 2 is where most indie launches go quiet.

I'm writing this at midnight on Day 1 of Skissify's launch. In 8 hours, the US east coast wakes up. Product Hunt's voting window is still open. The Hacker News thread has comments I haven't replied to. And the dopamine of "we're live!" is wearing off.

Here's exactly what I'm doing to keep the momentum.

---

## Why Day 2 Matters More Than Day 1

On launch day, you have novelty. You're allowed to post "WE'RE LIVE!" and people click because it's news.

On Day 2, novelty is gone. You have to earn the click.

But Day 2 has something Day 1 doesn't: **real data**. You know what actually resonated. You know which tweet got shares, which Reddit post got upvotes, which community sent real signups (not just lurkers).

That data is marketing gold, and almost nobody uses it.

---

## The Day 2 Playbook I'm Running

### 1. The "Real Numbers" Post (Not Vanity Metrics)

I'm writing a raw retrospective with actual numbers from Day 1:
- Signups (not "visitors")
- Sketches created
- Percentage via AI/MCP
- Countries
- Conversion rate to Pro

Real numbers earn trust. Even bad numbers earn trust. "We got 23 signups, 14 created at least one sketch, 4 used the MCP server" is more interesting than "thousands of people visited our site."

If you're building in public, Day 2 is the day to publish the honest post.

### 2. Reply to Every HN Comment

Hacker News comments have a long tail. A thread from Day 1 can stay active into Day 3 if you keep engaging. Each reply you write is a small bump back to the top of active discussions.

The rule: reply within 2 hours, be substantive, don't be defensive.

Someone says your wobble algorithm is just a sine wave? Explain why it isn't. Someone asks why JSON instead of a GUI? This is an invitation to write your best explanation of the design decision. Those replies become your best documentation.

### 3. Activate the Communities That Haven't Heard Yet

On Day 1, I posted to Twitter, Indie Hackers, HN, and a few subreddits.

Day 2 is for:
- **r/mcp** (20K members specifically about Model Context Protocol — this is our core community and I didn't post there Day 1)
- **r/AI_Agents** (500K members building agent stacks)
- **MCP Discord** (official Anthropic community for MCP developers)
- **r/LocalLLaMA** (LLM benchmark angle — which local model generates valid Skissify JSON?)

These communities care deeply about a tool that MCP servers can call. They aren't the same audience as r/SideProject. They'll engage differently and more technically.

### 4. The Day 2 Blog Post (Not an Update - A New Perspective)

Don't post "Day 2 update: still alive." Post something new that only exists because Day 1 happened.

My Day 2 blog post topic: "The audience I didn't build this for" — about homeowners using Skissify for contractor briefs. This story didn't exist until launch day. It's Day 2's genuine scoop.

Frame it as a discovery, not a milestone. "Here's something weird that happened" outperforms "here's our update" every time.

### 5. Screenshot the Unexpected

On Day 1, someone generated a dungeon map for their D&D campaign. Someone else used it to explain a kitchen renovation to their contractor. A geometry teacher created 30 spatial math exercises.

I'm screenshot-capturing every unexpected use case, getting permission to share, and turning them into social content. These are more compelling than anything I prepared.

---

## The Mistake Most Launches Make on Day 2

They treat Day 2 as a continuation of Day 1 messaging.

Day 1 is "here's what we built." Day 2 is "here's what happened when we built it." The frame shifts from product to story.

The story is always more interesting than the product.

---

## What I'm NOT Doing on Day 2

**Not refreshing analytics every 10 minutes.** The number will be whatever it will be. The work is what changes it.

**Not mass-emailing my whole network.** One targeted DM to someone who genuinely needs this is worth more than 100 blast messages.

**Not posting the same content twice.** Each platform gets content native to that platform. Reddit gets a "here's what I built and what happened" story. Twitter gets a sharp one-liner with data. LinkedIn gets the professional angle.

**Not stopping at 5pm.** US west coast engagement peaks at 10am PT, which is 7pm CET. Staying online through 10pm CET captures a different audience than the morning crowd.

---

## The Real Point

Day 2 isn't about maintaining momentum from Day 1. It's about building a second wave with new material.

The material is sitting in your Day 1 data, your HN thread, your DMs, your analytics. You have everything you need.

The only question is whether you show up to use it.
