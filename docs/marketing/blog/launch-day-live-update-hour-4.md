## What Launch Day Actually Feels Like

It's not what I expected.

I've read hundreds of "I launched on Product Hunt" threads. Most of them either describe a triumphant moment of validation or a cautionary tale of crushing indifference. Mine is neither.

It's quiet and loud at the same time.

The quiet part: most of the world doesn't know Skissify exists. The analytics dashboard refreshes every 30 seconds. You watch it. You try not to watch it. You watch it again.

The loud part: the few people who *do* find it — the MCP builders, the architects using Claude Desktop, the indie devs who've been waiting for exactly this — they go *deep*. Comments that take 5 minutes to read. DMs that feel like the beginning of a collaboration, not just feedback.

That's the thing nobody tells you: launch day isn't a single event. It's a day-long conversation with a handful of people who genuinely care about what you built.

---

## What's Working

**The MCP angle is resonating.** Every post that leads with "AI agents can draw now" gets more engagement than posts that lead with "JSON to sketch." The developer audience in 2026 is deep in the agentic workflow space. They get the problem immediately.

**The technical specificity helps.** "Multi-harmonic wobble" sounds jargony, but the devs who read it do a double-take. It signals this wasn't thrown together. It signals there was thought behind the rendering engine.

**The comparison posts drive traffic.** The "Skissify vs Excalidraw" framing (already in [the blog](./skissify-vs-excalidraw.md)) converts curiosity into visits better than pure "look what I built."

---

## What's Harder Than Expected

**Product Hunt timing.** Launching in Europe means the US audience — which dominates PH — is asleep for the first 6 hours. By the time they wake up, your listing is already several hours old. If you're a European founder launching on PH: aim for midnight PT, not midnight CET.

**HN is a coin flip.** The Show HN went up at 10:00. Whether it gets traction depends on whether the right person sees it in the first 20 minutes. That's mostly luck. I've got a good title and a strong first comment. The rest is out of my hands.

**Responding to everything is a full-time job.** I have templates for every response type (see [HN-RESPONSE-PLAYBOOK.md](../HN-RESPONSE-PLAYBOOK.md)). Without them, I'd have spent 3 hours crafting individual replies. With them, I can respond in 90 seconds and still sound like a human.

## The Unexpected Win

Someone DMed me at 10:30 to say they'd immediately added Skissify to their renovation planning workflow. Not a developer — a homeowner who found it through a Reddit thread and used it to sketch a kitchen layout for a contractor call.

That's not the audience I designed for. But it's real.

The MCP server was built for AI agents. But the web editor works for anyone who wants to sketch something quickly and send it to someone. The agent use case is the hook; the human use case is the long tail.

I hadn't fully thought through that until someone used it that way.

Building Skissify took months. Some of those months were slow. There's a period in every project where you're not sure if it's good or if you've just spent so long with it that you can't tell anymore.
