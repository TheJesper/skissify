# The 10 DMs That Will Matter More Than 10,000 Impressions Tomorrow

*Posted April 3, 2026 — the night before our Product Hunt launch*

---

Tomorrow Skissify goes live on Product Hunt.

I've spent three days building an impressive content calendar: blog posts, Reddit copy, LinkedIn articles, Twitter threads, Discord pitches. Cycle 124 of our marketing agent cycle just wrapped. Over 120 pieces of drafted content sit ready.

And none of it will matter as much as 10 personal messages.

## The Product Hunt Algorithm Is People

Product Hunt ranks by upvotes per unit of time. Not impressions. Not reach. Not SEO. Actual people clicking the orange arrow.

The platform is ruthless about launch velocity. Products that get 20 upvotes in the first hour surface to thousands of people through the newsletter and homepage. Products that get 3 upvotes in the first hour don't.

This means the first 30 minutes is decided by people who already know you — not strangers discovering you through a well-crafted tweet.

## The 10 DMs Protocol

Tonight (before midnight), I'm sending 10 personal messages. Not copy-paste. Not a bulk email. Ten messages, each one different.

The only rules:

**1. Name a specific reason it's relevant to them.**

Wrong: "Hey, I'm launching on PH tomorrow — would love your support!"

Right: "Hey, I remembered you were renovating your apartment last year — Skissify lets you describe a room layout and get a hand-drawn floor plan in seconds. Launching on Product Hunt tomorrow at 09:00 CET. Would be great if you could take a look."

The specific detail is the signal. It says: I thought about *you*, not just about upvotes.

**2. Tell them what to do and when.**

"Tomorrow, April 4, between 09:00 and 11:00 CET — that's our launch window. If you want to help, clicking the upvote arrow is the highest-value thing you can do."

Remove friction. Give a time and a specific action.

**3. One ask per message. No chains.**

Don't send a follow-up requesting a share. Don't ask them to tell their followers. One ask: upvote at launch time. People are more likely to do one thing than two things.

## Who to Pick

The 10 people who matter most for Product Hunt are not your biggest Twitter followers. They're people who:

- Will actually check their phone in the morning
- Have a reason to care about what you built
- Are willing to spend 20 seconds on a website to understand it
- Have a Product Hunt account (or will create one for you)

My list looks like this:

- 3 friends who work in AI or developer tools
- 2 architect/design contacts who have an actual reason to want floor plan software
- 2 indie hackers I've followed for years who ship regularly (they get it)
- 1 community friend from a Discord I've been active in
- 2 family members who are weirdly into home renovation content

That last one sounds like a joke. It isn't. Homeowners are one of Skissify's unexpected audiences. My mum is going to upvote tomorrow and she'll do it because she actually wants to draw her kitchen layout.

## What I'm Not Doing Tonight

I'm not writing another blog post that covers the same ground as the last 50 (this one is meta, that's different).

I'm not scheduling more tweets. We have 30 pre-written tweet templates. None of them will generate 10 upvotes. The 10 DMs will.

I'm not obsessing over the Product Hunt thumbnail one more time. It's good enough.

I'm not trying to get coverage from newsletters tonight. The window for newsletter pitches has passed. Tomorrow's emails are set.

## The Demo Video Problem

Full honesty: I still haven't recorded the demo video.

This has been P0 FOREVER in our marketing system since Cycle 1. I'm going to record it tomorrow morning at 08:00 CET before the launch window opens. 60 seconds. Screen capture of Claude Desktop generating a floor plan via MCP.

## What Product Hunt Is For

Product Hunt is not where users come from. It's where validation comes from.

The 500 upvotes we might get tomorrow aren't 500 future paying customers. They're 500 people saying: "this is a real thing that solved a real problem." That validation ripples. It shows up in HN comment threads. It gives journalists a hook ("Product Hunt featured"). It gives potential users social proof when they're on the fence.

So tomorrow isn't about conversions. It's about proof.

The 10 DMs are my stake in the ground that I care about the proof enough to actually ask for it personally.
