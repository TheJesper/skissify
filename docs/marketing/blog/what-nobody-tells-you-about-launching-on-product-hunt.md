# What Nobody Tells You About Launching on Product Hunt

*Author: Jesper | Category: BuildInPublic | Published: April 1, 2026*

---

I've read every "how to launch on Product Hunt" guide. There's a lot of advice about:
- Submitting your product the night before
- Getting an established hunter
- Preparing your first comment in advance
- Posting on social at exactly 12:01 AM PST

What the guides don't tell you:

---

## 1. The First Comment Is More Important Than the Description

Product Hunt's algorithm weights early engagement heavily. But more importantly, humans weight the founder's first comment as a trust signal.

A first comment that says "Hi PH! I'm the founder, here's the story, here's the thing I'm most proud of, here are the use cases I didn't expect — ask me anything" converts browsers into voters faster than any tagline.

Write the first comment like it's your single best chance to be a human being on a product review site.

---

## 2. Your Community Outperforms Cold Traffic by 10x

The 200 people who've been watching you build this thing will do more work on launch day than 2,000 cold eyes on a paid placement.

They'll write the first comments. They'll leave the nuanced reviews. They'll share it with the "you'd actually want to try this" framing.

If you haven't been building in public before your launch, you're launching without your biggest asset.

---

## 3. The Category You Choose Is the Competition You Get

Skissify could be "AI Tools," "Developer Tools," "Design Tools," or "Productivity."

Each category has different average daily launches, different typical upvote counts, and different user intent.

If you put a niche developer tool in "AI Tools" during a heavy launch day, you're competing against $50M-funded AI writing tools for the attention of non-technical voters.

Match the category to your actual power users, not your aspirational brand.

---

## 4. The US Evening Window Is Real

Most PH users are in the US. The 6-8pm PST window (3-5am next-day CET for European founders) is when the US evening engagement kicks in.

If you launch at 00:01 PST, you have 18 hours of US prime time ahead of you. If you launch at 12:00 PST, you have 6 hours before the day resets.

This is the one tactical piece of advice that's actually correct. Launch at the start of the PH day. Not the middle.

---

## 5. April 1 Is an Underrated Launch Date

Everyone thinks April 1 is a bad day to launch a real product.

Here's the contrarian thesis: it's actually a filter.

The people who click through to your April 1 product launch are making an active decision: *I'm going to verify if this is real.* That's intent. That's a user who wants to be convinced.

The ones who bounced because they assumed it was a joke were never going to be your users anyway.

I launched Skissify on April 1. Intentionally. The tagline is "Text in. Hand-drawn sketch out. MCP native. Not a joke."

We'll have the data by tomorrow.

---

## 6. Prep the Post-Mortem Before Launch

I know the result I'm hoping for. I know the questions I'll want to answer on April 2.

I wrote the post-mortem template before launch day. The sections are:
- The numbers (upvotes, visits, signups, renders in 24h)
- Did the April 1 filter theory hold?
- What I got wrong
- Which unexpected audience dominated
- What's next

Writing this in advance forces clarity about what success actually means. Not just "top 5 on PH today" but: what's the conversion rate from visitor to user? What's the engagement quality?

Whatever the numbers are tomorrow, the post-mortem will be honest. That post will live longer than the launch day ranking.

---

## 7. The Launch Is Not the Destination

Every "launch on Product Hunt" guide is actually about day one of a longer arc.

The real question is: what are you doing on April 2?

If the answer is "rest" — fine, you earned it. But the products that compound from a PH launch are the ones that treated day one as the beginning of a content, community, and outreach flywheel.

Blog posts citing PH performance. HN Show HN submissions with launch results. Follow-up tweets to people who commented. Email sequences to signups.

Product Hunt is an audience amplifier. The product still has to work after the amplification.
