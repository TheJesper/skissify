# Sunday Night: How to Close a Launch Week

*Published: Day 3 Sunday — March 29, 2026*
*Platform: Indie Hackers (primary), Substack, Dev.to*

---

**TL;DR:** Launch week is almost over. Most builders run out of energy by Sunday night. Here's what to do with the last 4 hours of it — and why they matter more than you think.

---

It's Sunday evening. The Product Hunt run is done. The Hacker News thread has cooled. Your launch tweet is 60 hours old.

This is the moment most founders check out.

That's a mistake.

Sunday night — specifically the 20:00–23:59 window — is when the US West Coast finishes its weekend and begins its Monday prep. It's when tech writers review what they bookmarked over the weekend. It's when Substack authors queue their Monday morning newsletter content.

If you want to make Monday's HN post land, do the work tonight.

---

## What "Closing a Launch Week" Actually Means

Closing a launch week isn't posting a celebration thread. It's three concrete things:

**1. Publish your real data.**
Not "we got good traction." Not "we're humbled by the response." Actual numbers: unique visitors, signups, sketches created, pro conversions, newsletter subscribers, HN rank, PH rank. Readers trust specificity. Vague success posts don't get shared.

**2. Identify the one unexpected thing.**
Every launch has one. For Skissify, it was non-developers. 30%+ of Day 1 signups described themselves as homeowners, dungeon masters, or educators — not AI engineers. That surprise became the centerpiece of every Day 2 post. Find your equivalent.

**3. Announce your first Week 2 move.**
The best way to keep launch momentum alive past Sunday is to give your audience something to watch for. A feature drop. A new integration. A blog post that drops Monday morning. You don't need to have it done — you just need to name it.

---

## The Human Mode Story (Skissify's Week 2 Hook)

Skissify launched as a JSON-first tool for AI developers. By Saturday, we knew that wasn't the only use case.

Homeowners were describing their kitchens to Claude and pasting the resulting JSON into Skissify to show contractors. Dungeon masters were using the blueprint paper style to generate maps. A geometry teacher emailed asking if she could use it for classroom exercises.

All of them hit the same wall: **the JSON barrier**.

So we're shipping Human Mode — a simple text input that describes your space in plain English, calls the API, and renders the sketch. No JSON required. It's not a pivot. It's a front door for the other 70% of the audience.

That's the Week 2 hook. And announcing it tonight is better than waiting until Monday morning.

## The Sunday Night Checklist

Here's what to do with the last 4 hours of launch week:

### 20:00 — Publish Your 72-Hour Retrospective
The definitive launch story: what worked, what didn't, the one surprise, the Week 2 direction. Publish on Indie Hackers first. Cross-post Dev.to. Link to it from Twitter with a simple "Day 3 is ending. Here's what happened." post.

### 21:00 — Send Newsletter Outreach (5 Targets)
If you haven't already: TLDR AI, The Rundown, Ben's Bites, Superhuman, Pointer.io. Each one gets a single paragraph: what the tool does, the surprise use case story, the Week 2 hook. Send from a real email address, not a tool. Short is better than complete.

### 22:00 — Prep Monday's HN Post
"Show HN: Skissify + Human Mode — describe a room, get a hand-drawn floor plan."

Write the body tonight. Sleep on it. Post Monday 09:00 CET when EU devs are at their desks and US East Coast is at breakfast.

The second Show HN is always harder to land — but Human Mode is a concrete new feature, not a re-post. That's the difference.

### 23:00 — Write One Tweet for Tomorrow
Not a launch tweet. Not an announcement. Just an honest observation:

> "Day 4 of launch. The developers I built it for are still using it. The homeowners I didn't build it for are still using it more. Not sure which is the better signal."

That kind of post at 09:00 Monday does more work than another product announcement.

---

## What Not to Do

- **Don't post a "Week 1 numbers" thread before you have them.** Placeholders in a "real numbers" post destroy the format.
- **Don't announce features you haven't built.** "Human Mode is coming" is fine. "Human Mode ships tomorrow" locks you in.
- **Don't go dark.** Even one short post Sunday night keeps the momentum alive. Going silent for 48 hours after a launch is the number-one way to lose it.

---

## The Week 2 Setup

The goal of Sunday night is one thing: make Monday feel like a continuation, not a restart.

If Monday's HN post goes up, if the newsletter outreach lands with one editor, if the Human Mode announcement gets 20 retweets — you haven't lost your launch week. You've extended it.

Most products get 3-5 days of organic momentum after a launch. The question is whether you use them.
