I'm a developer and I built [Skissify](https://skissify.com) primarily for AI agent workflows — but then homeowners started using it, and I realized I'd built something more broadly useful.

Here's the short version of what it does:

You describe a room in plain English. You get a hand-drawn floor plan sketch back in under 4 seconds. No software to install. No account required. Free.

Example: I typed this in the text box at [skissify.com/human](https://skissify.com/human):

> *Living room 5x6m. Sofa on the south wall facing north toward the TV unit. Coffee table in front of the sofa. Two armchairs on the east side angled toward the TV. Reading nook in the NE corner with a floor lamp. Sliding door to garden on the west wall.*

And got back a clean, hand-drawn floor plan with all of those elements labeled and positioned.

**Why hand-drawn and not photorealistic?** The rough style is intentional. When you're planning a renovation, rough sketches actually get better feedback than polished renders. People react to the *idea* rather than the *execution*. It's easier to say "can we move the sofa?" when the sketch is obviously a sketch.

**What people have used it for:**
- Planning room layouts before buying furniture
- Quickly sketching a renovation idea to share with a contractor
- Figuring out if a piece of furniture will fit the way they're imagining it
- Showing a partner what a layout change would look like without spending hours in a floor plan app

It works for any indoor space: bedrooms, kitchens, living rooms, home offices, basements.

The tool is at [skissify.com/human](https://skissify.com/human). You just type in a text box and hit generate. No account, no signup, free.

Happy to answer questions about how it works. Has anyone else experimented with AI tools for room planning?
