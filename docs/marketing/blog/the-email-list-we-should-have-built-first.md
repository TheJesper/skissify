# The Email List We Should Have Built on Day Zero

*A note to myself and other founders — March 28, 2026*

---

It's 48 hours post-launch. We have hundreds of signups, decent HN engagement, a viral Twitter thread, and community posts across a dozen subreddits.

We have zero email subscribers.

This is the most preventable mistake I made in the Skissify launch.

## What Happened

The launch checklist had "email newsletter" on it. I kept deprioritizing it. The reasoning went something like:
- "People will sign up for the app, not a newsletter"
- "Let me focus on getting traction first"
- "I can add email capture in Week 1"

All three of these are wrong.

**Wrong #1: "People will sign up for the app."**
Most people who find a new tool on launch day don't sign up immediately. They bookmark it. They share the link. They think "I'll try this later." Later never comes, because the bookmark gets buried and they forget.

An email address captures the "later" audience. The person who genuinely wants to come back but won't.

**Wrong #2: "Let me focus on traction first."**
Email capture *is* traction. PH upvotes expire. HN threads die. Subreddit posts age out. An email list compounds. The 50 people who give you their email on Day 1 are worth more than the 500 who upvote and disappear.

**Wrong #3: "I can add it in Week 1."**
Every day without email capture is permanent loss. Those hundreds of visitors from Friday? Most won't come back. There's no way to reach them. They saw the product, maybe liked it, and left. Gone.

## What I'm Adding Today

Three things:

**1. Homepage banner:**
"Get weekly sketches + build notes from the Skissify team →" [email input] [Subscribe]

Simple. Not a popup. Not a modal. Just a persistent offer at the bottom of the hero section.

**2. Post-sketch CTA:**
After someone creates and saves their first sketch, a gentle prompt: "Like what you made? Get a weekly digest of example sketches and integration guides."

This is the highest-intent moment. They've just experienced the product. If they don't subscribe here, they probably won't.

**3. Blog post footer:**
Every blog post ends with: "I write about building Skissify in public. Subscribe for weekly updates → [link]"

## What I'll Send

The first email writes itself. Subject line: "What happened when we launched Skissify (real numbers, real surprises)."

Content:
- Day 1 and Day 2 actual metrics (signups, sketches created, Pro conversions)
- The unexpected audiences (homeowners, dungeon masters, geometry teachers)
- What we're building next (Human Mode, TypeScript SDK, A2A support)
- Three example sketches generated this weekend

That email would have gone to several hundred people today. It's going to go to... however many people subscribe in the next few hours instead.

## The Lesson

The least glamorous part of a product launch is usually the highest-ROI part.

Product Hunt is glamorous. Hacker News is glamorous. Twitter threads are glamorous.

An email form is not glamorous. It's a box on a webpage where people type their email address. But it's the only channel that lets you send a message to someone who came, liked what they saw, and left — and bring them back.

Build the email list first. Everything else is borrowed attention.
